# Capstone rubric

*The capstone is "Build your own AI assistant." This rubric is for **encouragement and
reflection**, not grading.* There are no scores sent home. Use it to celebrate what each
kid achieved and to guide the Week 4 showcase.

## How to use it

- Fill one in per kid during/after the Week 4 showcase, or have kids **self-assess** it.
- Frame everything as "you did this!" not "you're missing this."
- Every kid who shows up and presents *something* has succeeded. The levels just help you
  give specific, genuine praise.

## The four things we look for

A great young-builder assistant shows growth in four areas — one per week:

### 1. Purpose (Week 1)
*Does the assistant have a clear job it's meant to help with?*

| Emerging | Solid | Stretch |
|----------|-------|---------|
| Has a general idea ("a helper bot") | Clear, specific purpose ("helps me practice Spanish words") | Purpose is specific *and* the kid can say who it's for and why it's useful |

### 2. Personality & prompting (Week 2)
*Did they shape how it talks, and can they steer it with prompts?*

| Emerging | Solid | Stretch |
|----------|-------|---------|
| Picked a vibe (e.g. "funny") | Wrote a system prompt that gives it a consistent voice | System prompt sets voice **and** rules (what it should/shouldn't do); kid can show prompting changing the output |

### 3. It runs in code (Week 3)
*Did they get their assistant working in the Colab notebook?*

| Emerging | Solid | Stretch |
|----------|-------|---------|
| Ran the notebook with help | Customized the persona in the notebook and got replies | Changed more than the persona (e.g. an extra rule, a different greeting) and can explain what a line does |

### 4. Smart & safe citizen (Week 4)
*Do they understand its risks and limits, and did they make it safer?*

| Emerging | Solid | Stretch |
|----------|-------|---------|
| Can name one risk (e.g. "it can be wrong") | Named a real risk **and** added one guardrail to their bot | Explains a guardrail they added *and* a limitation their assistant still has |

## Showcase reflection card (give to each kid)

A simple self-reflection kids fill in before they present:

> **My assistant is called:** ____________________
>
> **It helps people:** ____________________
>
> **The best thing about it is:** ____________________
>
> **One way it could go wrong is:** ____________________
>
> **One thing I made safer:** ____________________
>
> **If I had another week, I'd add:** ____________________

## Facilitator praise prompts

When each kid finishes presenting, say something specific. Sentence starters:

- "I noticed you made it… and that's a real builder move because…"
- "The way you handled [the bug / the weird answer / the prompt] showed…"
- "You explained [how it works / a risk] really clearly — that's exactly what an AI citizen
  does."

!!! success "The real goal"
    The point isn't a perfect bot. It's a kid who can **demo what they built, explain how it
    works, and name how it could go wrong**. Any kid who does that has nailed the capstone.
