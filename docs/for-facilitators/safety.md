# Safety, consent & parent letter

*Read this before running the club. Safety here is designed in, not bolted on.*

Kids will use real AI tools. That's the point — and it's also a responsibility. This page
covers the rules we follow, a guardian consent form, and a ready-to-send parent letter.

## The five club safety rules

These are taught to kids in Week 1 and posted in the room every session:

1. **No real secrets.** Never type your full name, address, school, passwords, or phone
   number into an AI tool. Use a first name or a made-up name.
2. **An adult is always nearby.** AI tools are used with the facilitator present and
   circulating. If something feels wrong, that's fine — it's not your fault.
3. **Flag it, don't hide it.** If a tool shows something scary, mean, or weird, tell the
   facilitator right away. You won't be in trouble.
4. **AI can be wrong.** Don't believe everything it says. Check important things with a
   real source or a trusted adult.
5. **Be kind with AI.** Don't use it to make mean things about real people. What you make
   says something about you.

## Facilitator responsibilities

- **You own the accounts.** Kids do not create personal accounts for AI tools. Use
  facilitator-managed accounts or no-login tools (see [Setup](setup.md)).
- **Supervise actively.** Circulate during hands-on time. Glance at screens. You're not
  policing — you're available.
- **Use kid-appropriate tools with filters on.** Choose tools whose terms permit supervised
  use with this age group and that have content filtering enabled.
- **Keep prompts personal-data-free.** Reinforce rule #1 every session.
- **Have the fallback ready.** If a tool misbehaves or shows something inappropriate, switch
  to the unplugged version of the activity (in each facilitator guide) without drama.
- **Protect the work.** Kids' saved work lives in a facilitator-controlled folder, not on
  public or personal third-party accounts.

## Data & privacy

- We collect the **minimum**: a first name (or nickname) per kid for organizing their work.
- Kids' creations are stored in a **facilitator-controlled** cloud folder, shared only with
  the kid's guardian on request.
- We do **not** create third-party AI-tool accounts in a child's name or with a child's
  email.
- Anything typed into an AI tool may be processed by that tool's provider — which is exactly
  why **rule #1** (no personal data) exists and is enforced.

## Guardian consent — what we ask permission for

The consent form asks a guardian to approve:

- [ ] Their child using **supervised, facilitator-managed AI tools** during sessions.
- [ ] Their child's **first name/nickname** being used to label their saved project work.
- [ ] **Webcam use in Week 1** for the Teachable Machine activity (images are used live in
      the browser to train a model and are **not** uploaded or stored by us).
- [ ] (Optional) Photos of the **Week 4 showcase** for the club's own records / promotion —
      this one is opt-in and separate.

## 📄 Ready-to-send parent letter

> Copy this into an email or print it. Replace the **[bracketed]** parts.

---

**Dear Parent/Guardian,**

Your child is invited to join the **AI Young Builders Club** — a four-week, one-hour-a-week
club where kids aged 11–13 learn how artificial intelligence really works by **building
their own AI assistant**. It runs on **[dates/times]** at **[location]**.

**What they'll do:** Over four weekends, kids will train a simple AI model, learn how
chatbots work, write a little real code in the browser, and learn how to use AI safely and
responsibly. Each child finishes with their own AI assistant project, which they'll present
in a friendly showcase on the final week.

**How we keep it safe:**

- All AI tools are used **with adult supervision** and on **accounts we manage** — your
  child will not create personal accounts.
- Kids are taught from day one to **never enter personal information** (real full names,
  addresses, passwords, etc.) into any tool.
- We use **age-appropriate tools with content filters**, and we have offline backup
  activities ready.
- Your child's project work is stored in a **private folder we control**, available to you
  on request.

**What we need from you:** Please complete the short consent form below so your child can
take part. If you have any questions, contact **[facilitator name]** at **[contact]**.

We're excited to help **[child's name]** become a confident, thoughtful young builder.

Warm regards,
**[Facilitator name]**, AI Young Builders Club
