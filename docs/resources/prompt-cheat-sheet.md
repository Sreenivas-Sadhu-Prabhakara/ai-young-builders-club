# Prompting cheat sheet

*Print this double-sided. Kids use it in Weeks 2, 3, and 4.*

A **prompt** is what you type to tell an AI what you want. The AI can't read your mind — it
can only go on the words you give it. Better prompt → better result. Here's how to write
good ones.

## The 4 ingredients of a great prompt

Think **R-T-F-C**: *Role, Task, Format, Check.*

| Ingredient | What it means | Example |
|------------|---------------|---------|
| 🎭 **Role** | Tell it *who to be* | "You are a friendly science tutor for an 11-year-old." |
| 🎯 **Task** | Say *exactly* what you want | "Explain why the sky is blue." |
| 📐 **Format** | Say *how* you want the answer | "In 3 short bullet points, no big words." |
| ✅ **Check** | Add a rule or a check | "If you're not sure, say so. Don't make things up." |

Put together:

> *"You are a friendly science tutor for an 11-year-old. Explain why the sky is blue in 3
> short bullet points, no big words. If you're not sure, say so."*

## Before & after

| 😴 Weak prompt | 🚀 Strong prompt |
|----------------|------------------|
| "Tell me about dogs." | "List 5 fun facts about dogs for a kid, one sentence each." |
| "Write a story." | "Write a 6-sentence funny story about a robot who's scared of cats." |
| "Help with my homework." | "Explain how to add fractions, step by step, with one easy example." |
| "Make a picture of a dragon." | "A cute cartoon green dragon reading a book, friendly, bright colors, simple background." |

## Power moves

- **Ask it to think step by step.** "Explain step by step" often gets better, clearer
  answers.
- **Give an example of what you want.** "Make 3 more like this: …" works great.
- **Set the audience.** "Explain it like I'm 11" changes everything.
- **Say what you DON'T want.** "No scary parts," "keep it under 50 words."
- **Iterate.** Your first prompt is a draft. Reply with "make it shorter / funnier / simpler"
  and watch it improve.
- **Ask it to check itself.** "Are you sure? Double-check that." can catch mistakes.

## Prompting for images 🎨

Image AIs love **describe-y** prompts. Stack these:

```
[subject] + [doing what] + [style] + [mood/colors] + [background]
```

Example:
> *"A small orange robot watering a plant, cartoon style, cheerful, pastel colors, plain
> background."*

Tips:
- More specific = more control. Vague = surprise.
- Try the **same idea in different styles**: "cartoon," "pixel art," "watercolor," "3D."
- Don't expect perfect hands or text — image AIs are famously bad at both!

## Writing your assistant's **system prompt** (Week 2 capstone)

A system prompt is your assistant's "character sheet." A good one for the club has:

```
You are [NAME], a [PERSONALITY] assistant that helps with [PURPOSE].
You always [HOW IT TALKS — e.g. keep answers short and friendly].
You never [A RULE — e.g. say anything mean, or pretend to be a real person].
If you don't know something, you say so instead of making it up.
```

Filled-in example:

> *"You are Sparky, a cheerful and patient assistant that helps kids practice their times
> tables. You always give one short hint before the answer and use encouraging words. You
> never give the answer immediately or say anything mean. If you're unsure, you say so."*

## Remember (always!)

!!! warning "The two golden rules"
    1. **No secrets in prompts** — no real full names, addresses, passwords, school names.
    2. **AI can be confidently wrong** — check anything important with a real source or a
       trusted adult.
