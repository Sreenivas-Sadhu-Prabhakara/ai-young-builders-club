# Week 2 · Talking machines

!!! abstract "At a glance"
    - **Big idea:** Chatbots work by **predicting the next word** — your **prompt** steers
      them, and they can be **confidently wrong** (hallucination) and **biased** (because they
      learned from human-made examples).
    - **Hands-on "wow":** Turn a weak prompt into a strong one, catch the bot inventing fake
      facts, and make images in wildly different styles.
    - **Capstone step:** Give your assistant a **personality** and write its first **system
      prompt**.
    - **Kids leave saying:** *"A chatbot just predicts the next word — and my prompt is the
      steering wheel."*

## Why this week

Last week kids felt the big truth: *AI learns from examples.* This week we point that same
truth at the AIs they actually talk to. Chatbots feel magic — like there's a little person
inside — so the wonder this week is **deflating that magic in the best way**: it's just
**predicting the next word**, billions of times, super fast. Once a kid *feels* that, two
grown-up ideas land for free: chatbots can be **confidently wrong** (hallucination) and
**unfair** (bias) — a straight callback to Week 1's *garbage in, garbage out*. And the
antidote to both is a skill they can practice today: **writing better prompts**.

## Learning goals

By the end of the hour, each kid can:

- Say, in their own words, that a chatbot works by **predicting the next word**.
- Use the **R-T-F-C** method (Role, Task, Format, Check) to turn a weak prompt into a strong
  one.
- Spot a **hallucination** — and explain *why* the bot makes things up.
- Explain that chatbots can be **biased** because they learned from human-made examples
  (*garbage in, garbage out*).
- Write a first **system prompt** giving their capstone assistant a personality.

## The capstone this week

Last week each kid chose **what their assistant helps with**. This week it gets a soul. Kids
design their assistant's **personality** and write its first **system prompt** using the
template from the [prompting cheat sheet](../resources/prompt-cheat-sheet.md#writing-your-assistants-system-prompt-week-2-capstone):

> *"You are [NAME], a [PERSONALITY] assistant that helps with [PURPOSE]. You always… You
> never… If you're unsure, you say so."*

They write it on their handout; the facilitator **saves it** (folder or photo). In Week 3,
this exact system prompt is what brings their assistant to life in real code — so don't let
it walk out the door un-saved.

## Go Further (for kids who race ahead)

- **Politely** try to confuse the bot — ask a trick or impossible question and watch how it
  wriggles. (No rude stuff — club rule #5.)
- Give the **same prompt to two different AIs** and compare the answers. Which is better? Why?
- Run the **same image prompt in 3 styles** ("cartoon," "pixel art," "watercolor") and line
  up the results.

## Catch-up path *(for anyone who missed it later)*

In 5 minutes: play one round of "predict the next word," hear the one big idea (*a chatbot
predicts the next word; your prompt steers it*), see one hallucination caught live, and write
your assistant's **system prompt** on the handout. You're caught up.
