# Week 4 · Smart & safe AI citizen + Showcase

!!! abstract "At a glance"
    - **Big idea:** AI is a **power tool** — like a power drill, it's not good or bad; *how
      people use it* is what matters.
    - **Hands-on "wow":** Kids try to **break their own assistant** (red-team it) — then add a
      **guardrail** so it behaves. Ethics, applied to their own project.
    - **Capstone step:** The **SHOWCASE** — every kid demos what they built.
    - **Kids leave saying:** *"AI is powerful, so I use it wisely — I can name a risk and a
      guardrail I added."*

## Why this week

This is the final week, and it does double duty. First, kids learn to be **good AI citizens**:
AI can be confidently wrong, it can be unfair, it can fake real things, and it remembers what
you type. None of that is scary if you know it — it's just how to use a powerful tool well.
Then comes the part they've worked toward for a month: the **showcase**, where each kid shows
off the assistant they built. We end on celebration, not a lecture.

## Learning goals

By the end of the hour, each kid can:

- Explain that **AI is a power tool** — its impact depends on how people use it.
- **Name one risk** of AI (wrong, unfair, fakes, or privacy) in their own words.
- **Describe one guardrail** they added to their own assistant and show it working.
- **Present their assistant** to the group: what it does, how it works, one risk, one safety
  thing they made.

## The capstone this week

This is the finish line. Each kid **presents their assistant** to the group (and parents, if
invited). Using the reflection card from the [rubric](../for-facilitators/rubric.md), they
share: what it's called, who it helps, the best thing about it, **one way it could go wrong**,
and **one thing they made safer**. The rubric is for *encouragement* — every kid who presents
something has succeeded. The job isn't a perfect bot; it's a kid who can demo what they built,
explain how it works, and name how it could go wrong.

## Go Further (for kids who race ahead)

- Add a **second guardrail** to their assistant and test both (e.g. "stays on topic" *and*
  "never says anything mean").
- Write a one-line **"warning label"** for their bot: *"This assistant is great at ___, but
  don't trust it for ___."*
- Try the ethics primer's **bias try-it** (ask an image AI for "a doctor" a few times) and
  report what they noticed back to the group.

## Catch-up path *(for anyone joining only the final week)*

In ~5 minutes: hear the one big idea (*AI is a power tool — use it wisely*), pick a quick
**purpose + name** for an assistant on a fresh personality sheet, and you still **present an
idea** in the showcase. You don't need a finished bot to be a builder — you need an idea you
can explain. Welcome aboard, and congratulations on finishing strong.
