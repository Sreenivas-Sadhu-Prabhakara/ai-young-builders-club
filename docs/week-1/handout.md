## 🤖 What even *is* AI?

> **Today's big idea:** AI **learns from examples** — it's not a list of rules a human typed.
> It's a super-powered **pattern spotter**.

### 3 words to know

| Word | What it means |
|------|---------------|
| **Training** | Showing an AI lots of examples |
| **Model** | The trained "brain" you get at the end |
| **Training data** | The examples you showed it |

---

## 🦾 Activity: Train your own AI!

Go to **teachablemachine.withgoogle.com** → Get Started → **Image Project** → Standard image model.

**Your mission:**

- [ ] Make **2–3 classes** and rename them (e.g. `Thumbs Up`, `Thumbs Down`, `Nothing`)
- [ ] Record about **30 webcam examples** for each class (hold the pose!)
- [ ] Click **Train Model**
- [ ] Test it — wave around and watch the bars move! 🎉

**Experiment & write what you notice:**

When I added *more* and *more varied* examples, my model got... ___________________________

When I gave it only *one* kind of example, it... __________________________________________

> 💡 **Big lesson:** Good, varied examples = good AI. Bad examples = bad AI.
> *("Garbage in, garbage out!")*

**🚀 Go Further:** Try 3+ classes. Or give it bad examples on purpose, watch it fail, then fix it!

## 🔒 The Club Safety Rules

1. **No secrets** — never type my real full name, address, or passwords into an AI.
2. **A grown-up is always nearby.**
3. **Flag it, don't hide it** — if something's weird, tell the facilitator.
4. **AI can be wrong** — I check important things.
5. **Be kind with AI.**
