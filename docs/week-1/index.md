# Week 1 · What even *is* AI?

!!! abstract "At a glance"
    - **Big idea:** AI **learns from examples** — it's a super-powered pattern spotter, not a
      list of rules a human typed.
    - **Hands-on "wow":** Train a working AI model in the browser in ~5 minutes with
      Teachable Machine.
    - **Capstone step:** Choose your assistant's **purpose** — what will it help with?
    - **Kids leave saying:** *"I trained an AI, and I know it learns from examples."*

## Why this week comes first

Before any vocabulary, kids need one true gut-feeling: *AI learns from examples I give it.*
Everything else in the club — chatbots, code, ethics — sits on top of that. So we let them
**train a model with their own hands** before we explain a single term. The wonder comes
from doing it; the words land afterward.

## Learning goals

By the end of the hour, each kid can:

- Say, in their own words, that AI **learns from examples** instead of following typed rules.
- Point to **training data** and explain that good/varied examples make a better model
  (*garbage in, garbage out*).
- Name what their **capstone assistant** will help with (its purpose).
- State the club's **safety rules**, especially "no secrets."

## What you'll need

- One laptop per kid (or pairs), webcams on, modern browser.
- Projector for the facilitator.
- [Teachable Machine](https://teachablemachine.withgoogle.com) — no account needed.
- Printed [Week 1 handout](handout.md) (one per kid) and the
  [glossary](../resources/glossary.md) handy.
- The five [safety rules](../for-facilitators/safety.md#the-five-club-safety-rules) on the
  wall/screen.

## The capstone this week

This is the week the project is born. Each kid decides **what their AI assistant will help
with** — a homework buddy, a joke bot, a pet-care helper, a Minecraft tips bot, anything
that excites them. They write it on their handout; the facilitator keeps it. Everything in
Weeks 2–4 builds on this choice.

## Go Further (for kids who race ahead)

- Train a model with **three or more** classes and see if it gets confused.
- Deliberately give it **bad/lopsided examples** and watch it fail — then fix it. (This is a
  sneak preview of *bias*, coming in Week 2/4.)

## Catch-up path *(for anyone who missed it later)*

In 5 minutes: watch the facilitator train a quick Teachable Machine model, hear the one big
idea (*AI learns from examples*), and pick your assistant's purpose. You're caught up.
