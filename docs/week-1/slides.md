### Slide 1 — Title

> # What even *is* AI? 🤖
> AI Young Builders Club · Week 1

**Say:** "By the end of today, every one of you will have **trained your own AI** — and
you'll start building your very own AI assistant."

---

### Slide 2 — Two kinds of computer problems

> **Computers have always been great at RULES.**
> 7 + 12 · sort these names · is this number even?
>
> **But these are hard with rules:**
> Is this a cat or a dog? · Is this person smiling? · Is this review happy or angry?

**Say:** "You can't write a rule for 'what makes a cat a cat.' So how do computers do it
now?" (Let them guess.)

---

### Slide 3 — The big idea: learn from examples

> ## AI learns from EXAMPLES, not rules.
> Show it thousands of cats 🐱🐱🐱 → it figures out "cat-ness" by itself.

**Say:** "This is the whole secret of modern AI. We don't tell it the rules. We show it
examples, and it finds the pattern. That's called **machine learning**."

---

### Slide 4 — The words we'll use

> - **Training** = showing the AI lots of examples
> - **Model** = the trained 'brain' you get at the end
> - **Training data** = the examples themselves

**Say:** "Three words. Training is the practice. The model is what you get. Training data is
the examples. You'll make all three in a few minutes."

---

### Slide 5 — Garbage in, garbage out

> ## A model is only as good as its examples.
> 🍎 Only showed it *red* apples? It might not recognize a *green* one.

**Say:** "If your examples are bad or lopsided, your AI is too. Remember this — it comes back
when we talk about AI being unfair. **Good examples = good AI.**"

---

### Slide 6 — Your mission

> ## Train an AI to recognize YOU
> 1. Make 2–3 classes (thumbs up / thumbs down / nothing)
> 2. Record ~30 webcam examples each
> 3. Train it ▶️
> 4. Test it live!

**Say:** "Go to Teachable Machine. Your goal: build a model that can tell your poses apart.
More varied examples = better. Ready? Build!"

---

**Say:** "Homework helper? Joke bot? Pet-care helper? Game tips? You decide. Write it on your
sheet — you're the boss of your AI."
